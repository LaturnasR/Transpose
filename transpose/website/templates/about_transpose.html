WHAT IS TRANSPOSE?

  Transpose is a website that acts as a supplemental tool for students, especially Grade 7 students, in learning a specific topic namely: "Translating English phrases to mathematical expressions". The tool does exactly what the topic says, it translates english phrases inputted by the user, then outputs its mathematical expression equivalent.

WHY TRANSPOSE?

  According to Programme for International Student Assessment or PISA's statistics in 2018, the mean score of 15 year-old Filipinos' performance in secondary level mathematics is 353, that is 136 points lower than the average of 489 points among 77 countries participating in PISA, making the Philippines ranked 75th or the 3rd lowest performing country. According to the studies, one of the students' hindrance in learning mathematics is comprehending word problems. Students are having difficulties formulating these problems to turn into math expressions for easier solving.

  Based on DepEd’s K-12 Basic Education Curriculum, the translation of English phrases to mathematical phrases and vice versa are mostly encountered by Grade 7 students. Although online tools that solve math problems exist, they are either not broad enough to perceive problems involving multiple operators or complex enough to perceive simple structured phrases that are commonly used in grade 7 curriculum. This where Transpose comes in, a supplemental tool that caters to the phrases encountered in Grade 7 that would aid the students translate them sensibly.

HOW DOES IT WORK?

  Transpose accepts a maximum of one (1) English sentence that contain number values and arithmetic operations derivable from keywords. Appropriate grammar and syntax is needed for the sentence to be optimally translated. Transpose is case insensitive.

  Accepted inputs include:
  Number values in numerical form (e.g "1" , "2") or standard number words (e.g "one", "two")
  Words with an equivalent arithmetic operation

  Transpose is unable to accept or recognize the following inputs:
  Worded problems that contain scenarios
  Altered words that represent numbers or arithmetic operations (s!x, tw3lve, sum_)
  Words with incorrect spellings
