 Learn


  Worded Mathematical Problems follow the order of operations the way they are written, unless stated that one operation precedes the other. A simple step-by-step guide to follow would be:


1. Read the problem

2. Highlight the question (example, “The sum of ... “, “The quotient of ...”)

3. Identify and take note of the keywords and givens (e.g, “Twice the difference of 12 and 7 )

4. Solve the problem using the details sorted out in steps 2 and 3.



  Certain words symbolize operations. For example, the addition operation can be symbolized by the phrases "the sum of ..." , "the total of .. ", "combined", or "the tally of ..." , indicating the grand total of numbers combined. On the other hand, words like "difference" or "less than" indicate that the operation to be done is subtraction. Multiplication can be represented by the words "product", "times" and by using modified number words like "once", "twice" and "thrice" to indicate the number of times a variable would be multiplied. (e.g twice the sum of 3 and 5 = 2 * ( 3 + 5 ) ). Division can be identified by using the words "quotient", "remainder of ", and "divided by" .
