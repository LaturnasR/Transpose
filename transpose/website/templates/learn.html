Learn

English phrases or sentences can be translated easily into their corresponding mathematical expressions or equations by following these four simple steps:

1. Read

Read and understand the problem. Assign variables for unknown quantities.

2. Identify

Identify which words signify as the operands and which words signify as operators.

3. Analyze

Analyze each operator keywords and apply their meaning to their corresponding operands.

4. Convert

Convert the words into mathematical symbols and arrange them based on your analyzation.

1. Read

Read the following problem: Twice the sum of a number and twelve.

First, we look for words indicating an unknown quantity. The word 'number' does not indicate any value unlike the word 'twelve'. Hence, we assign it a variable 'x'. Doing so, will change our phrase to: Twice the sum of x and twelve. Notice that the word "a" was also replaced along with the word 'number' as it does not have any significant meaning.

Note: A variable is used to represent an unknown quantity and can be represented as any of the lower-case letters in the alphabet.

2. Identify

Next, we have to identify the keywords in our phrase.

Twice the sum of x and twelve.

Operator keywords are words that indicate math operations, while operands are numbers to be operated on by operators. In our case, we have two operator keywords namely: 'Twice' and 'the sum of' (highlighted in green). And two operands: 'x' and 'twelve' (highlighted in blue).

3. Analyze

Now it is time to apply the meaning of our identified operator keywords. First is the word 'twice', which is a mathematical operation that means to double or to multiply its operand by two. However, this operator's operand (the sum of x and 12) is yet to be solved, meaning, the other operator will be performed first. 'The sum of' keyword simply means that its operands are to be added together. Gathering our analyzations, we can imply that the idea of the phrase is: add x and twelve together, then multiply their result by two. '

4. Convert

Lastly, we just have to convert the idea into mathematical symbols.

add x and twelve → x + 12

multiply the result by two → 2 * (x + 12)

The pair of parentheses () is used to group an operation to indicate that it is to be performed first. If a grouped operation has another grouped operation inside, for example: '2 + (4 / (2 + 2))', then the innermost operation (2 + 2) will be performed first.

Note: The symbol used for multiplication is asterisk (*) to avoid confusion with the variable 'x'.
