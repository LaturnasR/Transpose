 How Does It Work


  There are two algorithms in play: Pre-processing and Conversion. The Pre-processing algorithm denoises and simplies the input. While the Conversion algorithm converts the preprocessed input into a translation output.


To understand the algorithms better, let's take a look at this example:

Three diminished by the sum of a number and four


Pre-Processing


Tokenization and POS Tagging

Through Preprocessing's tokenization and parts of speech (POS) tagging, each word will be categorized.

Three NUM diminished VERB by ADP the DET sum NOUN of ADP a DET number NOUN and CONJ four NOUN


Lemmatization

Words will then be lemmatized (reduced into their root word).

Three NUM diminish ADJ by ADP the DET sum NOUN of ADP a DET number NOUN and CONJ four NOUN


Filtering stop words

Then, stop words (words that bear no significant meaning) are removed.

Three NUM diminish ADJ sum NOUN number NOUN and CONJ four NOUN


Standardization

Last step for Preprocessing, keywords are standardized into its basic concept.

Three NUM minus ADJ sum NOUN number NOUN and CONJ four NOUN


Conversion


Number Conversion

Words that indicate numbers will be converted into their integer counterparts.

3 NUM minus ADJ sum NOUN number NOUN and CONJ 4 NOUN


Variables

Unknown words (words not defined in our algorithm), become variables.

3 NUM minus ADJ sum NOUN x NOUN and CONJ 4 NOUN


Interpretation

Using a defined context-free grammar and imposing a parse tree. The algorithm is able to identify and interpret the sentence structure of the given input. See sample interpretation below:


['3', 'minus', ('x', 'sum', '4') ]


3 minus (x sum 4)


3 - (x + 4)
